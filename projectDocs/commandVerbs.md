This is a nuanced request because the HSC marks responses based on **quality of performance** against a rubric (marking guideline), not just the question type.

However, we can map this using the **Common Grade Scale** principles used in NESA marking centres.

**How to read the "Marking Breakdown" in this list:**
*   **Typical Mark Range:** The total marks usually allocated to this verb (e.g., 1–3 marks).
*   **The Band Mapping:** If a student attempts a high-value question (e.g., 8 marks) but provides a weak response, their mark places them in a lower Band for that specific skill.

---

### Level 1: Retrieving & Recalling (The Basics)
**Typical Mark Range:** **1 – 2 Marks**
These questions are "all or nothing" or very short. They leave little room for partial credit based on depth.

**1. Identify | 2. Recall | 3. Define | 4. Extract | 5. Recount**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 2** (Elementary Knowledge)
*   **Marking Breakdown:**
    *   **1/1 or 2/2:** Indicates **Band 2/3** ability. You did the basics correctly.
    *   **0/1:** Indicates **Band 1** (Failure to meet minimum standard).
*   **Insight:** You cannot get a Band 6 *result* here, only a Band 6 *prerequisite*. If you miss these, you are capped at Band 2.

---

### Level 2: Comprehending & Describing
**Typical Mark Range:** **3 – 5 Marks**
These allow for some differentiation. A student who writes a lot but says little will fall into the lower marks here.

**6. Outline | 7. Describe | 8. Clarify | 9. Summarise | 10. Classify**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 3** (Sound Knowledge)
*   **Marking Breakdown (e.g., out of 4 marks):**
    *   **1/4:** **Band 1/2.** Student identified terms but failed to describe features.
    *   **2/4:** **Band 2/3.** Student outlined briefly or listed features (incomplete description).
    *   **3/4:** **Band 3.** Sound description, but missed some detail or clarity.
    *   **4/4:** **Band 4.** Comprehensive description.
*   **Insight:** To get full marks here, you don't need to analyse, you just need to be **thorough**. A "Describe" response will never get marks in the Band 6 range on a higher-order question, but on a 4-mark question, it represents the ceiling.

---

### Level 3: Applying & Demonstrating
**Typical Mark Range:** **3 – 6 Marks**
Calculations vary wildly by subject (Maths vs Science), but generally:

**11. Calculate | 12. Apply | 13. Demonstrate | 14. Construct**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 4** (Application of Knowledge)
*   **Marking Breakdown (e.g., out of 5 marks):**
    *   **1–2/5:** **Band 2.** Correct formula/method chosen, but execution failed.
    *   **3/5:** **Band 3.** "Sound" attempt. Correct substitution or method, but calculation error or missing unit.
    *   **4/5:** **Band 4.** Correct application, minor error in significant figures or notation.
    *   **5/5:** **Band 5/6.** Flawless execution.
*   **Insight:** In subjects like Physics or Economics, "Apply" is the gatekeeper. If you score 2/5 here, you are stuck in the "Rote Learning" bands (1–3).

---

### Level 4: Analysing & Connecting
**Typical Mark Range:** **5 – 8 Marks**
This is where the rubric splits students significantly.

**15. Compare | 16. Contrast | 17. Distinguish | 18. Explain | 19. Interpret | 20. Deduce | 21. Extrapolate | 22. Predict | 23. Analyse | 24. Examine | 25. Account for**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 5** (Thorough / Relational Knowledge)
*   **Marking Breakdown (e.g., out of 8 marks):**
    *   **1–2/8:** **Band 1/2.** Identifies the topic but simply lists facts. No cause-and-effect shown.
    *   **3–4/8:** **Band 3.** Describes the components separately ("A is this, B is that") but does not connect them (Analysis).
    *   **5–6/8:** **Band 4.** Explains the relationship or "how/why" clearly. Good standard.
    *   **7–8/8:** **Band 5/6.** Analyses the *implications* of the relationship. High level of detail and logic.
*   **Insight:** The most common mistake is responding to "Explain" (Level 4) with "Describe" (Level 2). On an 8-mark question, a perfect Description will cap out at roughly **3 or 4 marks**.

---

### Level 5: Synthesising & Arguing
**Typical Mark Range:** **8 – 15 Marks**
These questions usually require structure (Intro, Body, Conclusion).

**26. Discuss | 27. Propose | 28. Investigate | 29. Synthesise**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 5/6 Borderline**
*   **Marking Breakdown (e.g., out of 10 marks):**
    *   **1–3/10:** **Band 2.** Retells the story or lists facts. No argument.
    *   **4–6/10:** **Band 3/4.** One-sided discussion. "Describes" points for, but ignores points against.
    *   **7–8/10:** **Band 5.** "Sound" discussion. Covers both sides (for/against) clearly.
    *   **9–10/10:** **Band 6.** "Sophisticated" discussion. Synthesises points to form a new conclusion or judgment.
*   **Insight:** To jump from 7/10 (Band 5) to 10/10 (Band 6), the student must not just list points, but weave them together to prove a point.

---

### Level 6: Evaluating & Judging (Most Difficult)
**Typical Mark Range:** **10 – 20+ Marks** (Essays / Extended Responses)
This is the ultimate discriminator. This is where the state ranks are decided.

**30. Assess | 31. Evaluate | 32. Appreciate | 33. Justify | 34. Recommend | 35. Critically Analyse**

*   **Target Standard:** **Band 6** (Extensive / Critical Knowledge)
*   **Marking Breakdown (e.g., out of 20 marks):**
    *   **1–5/20:** **Band 1/2.** Elementary. Identifies the topic, writes generally, perhaps irrelevant info.
    *   **6–9/20:** **Band 3.** Descriptive. Tells you *what* happened in great detail, but never judges *how well* it worked.
    *   **10–14/20:** **Band 4.** Explanatory. Explains cause and effect well. Uses the word "effective" but doesn't prove it with criteria.
    *   **15–17/20:** **Band 5.** Analytical. Uses criteria to judge. Strong logic. Good structure.
    *   **18–20/20:** **Band 6.** Evaluative/Critical. Integrates a sophisticated judgment throughout the whole response. Nuanced, considers limitations of the evidence, highly polished.
*   **Insight:** A "Describe" response to a 20-mark "Evaluate" question will usually score a maximum of **8–10/20**. You cannot pass the mid-point of an essay mark without using the "Judgment" verbs (e.g., "highly effective," "limited success," "crucial impact").
