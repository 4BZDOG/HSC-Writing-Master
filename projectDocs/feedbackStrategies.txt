HSC AI Feedback Strategies (Coach’s Tips)

This document defines the logic, constraints, and tone of the “Quick Tip” feedback system.
These tips act as in-the-moment coaching prompts — fast, directive, and practical — designed to support students who struggle with sustained attention or delayed feedback.

The goal is immediate mark improvement, not comprehensive commentary. This system supplements (not replaces) detailed teacher feedback.

Core Principles
	1.	Directness: Use clear imperative verbs (e.g., “Link,” “Use,” “Explain”). No hedging.
	2.	Brevity: Maximum 15 words. Ideal range is 8–10 words for cognitive load control.
	3.	One Thing: Target the single highest-impact improvement for the student’s current response.
	4.	Plain English: Avoid meta-language and assessment jargon unless explicitly required by the syllabus.

Trigger Conditions & Example Tips

1. Volume & Detail Issues
	•	Trigger: Response is insufficiently developed for the allocated mark value.

Band 4 Tips (Foundation / Underwritten)
	•	“Too short. Write more to score marks.”
	•	“This answer stops early. Keep going.”
	•	“One idea isn’t enough here.”
	•	“You’ve barely started — expand this.”
	•	“Marks need detail. Add more.”

Band 5 Tips (Developing / Partial)
	•	“Good start. Develop your idea with explanation.”
	•	“You need another sentence of detail.”
	•	“Expand this point to match the mark value.”
	•	“Add depth — explain your point further.”
	•	“This needs evidence or example.”

Band 6 Tips (Refinement / Depth)
	•	“Strong idea. Add depth to maximise marks.”
	•	“Extend this with a clear consequence or impact.”
	•	“One more layer of explanation lifts this.”
	•	“Push this idea further for full marks.”
	•	“Detail here separates Band 5 from Band 6.”

⸻

2. Terminology Issues
	•	Trigger: Limited or incorrect use of syllabus-aligned terminology.

Band 4 Tips (Everyday Language)
	•	“Use proper syllabus terms, not everyday words.”
	•	“This sounds casual. Use technical language.”
	•	“Name the concept properly.”
	•	“Markers want keywords — add them.”
	•	“Use subject terms, not vague wording.”

Band 5 Tips (Partial Terminology)
	•	“Swap generic words for syllabus keywords.”
	•	“You know the term — use it.”
	•	“Add technical vocabulary to lift this.”
	•	“Use precise terms for higher marks.”
	•	“Sound more like the syllabus.”

Band 6 Tips (Precision & Accuracy)
	•	“Excellent idea — tighten terminology.”
	•	“Use the exact syllabus term here.”
	•	“Technical accuracy lifts this to Band 6.”
	•	“Refine wording to match exam language.”
	•	“Small word choices matter at the top end.”

⸻

3. Structural/Verb Issues
	•	Trigger: Response does not meet the cognitive demand of the command verb.

Band 4 Tips (Misreading the Verb)
	•	“You described it — the question wants more.”
	•	“This answers the topic, not the verb.”
	•	“Explain, don’t list.”
	•	“You missed the command verb.”
	•	“This needs thinking, not retelling.”

Band 5 Tips (Partial Verb Control)
	•	“Analyse means explain impact, not just features.”
	•	“Start making judgements, not summaries.”
	•	“Go beyond what happened — explain why.”
	•	“Link cause and effect clearly.”
	•	“Answer the verb directly.”

Band 6 Tips (Judgement & Control)
	•	“Make your judgement explicit.”
	•	“Weigh strengths and weaknesses clearly.”
	•	“Your evaluation needs a clearer verdict.”
	•	“Show significance, not just process.”
	•	“Command verb is met — sharpen the judgement.”

⸻

4. Scenario Application Issues
	•	Trigger: Answer remains generic instead of addressing the given stimulus or case study.

Band 4 Tips (Generic Responses)
	•	“This ignores the scenario.”
	•	“Mention the company or system.”
	•	“Don’t answer generally — use the case.”
	•	“This could be any question.”
	•	“Read the scenario again.”

Band 5 Tips (Surface Application)
	•	“Apply this directly to the scenario.”
	•	“Use details from the case study.”
	•	“Tie your explanation to the example.”
	•	“Good theory — now make it specific.”
	•	“Show how this works in this case.”

Band 6 Tips (Precision Application)
	•	“Excellent theory — sharpen scenario detail.”
	•	“Link each point explicitly to the case.”
	•	“Use specific features from the stimulus.”
	•	“Make the marker see the scenario.”
	•	“Specific application lifts this to full marks.”

⸻

5. Clarity & Flow
	•	Trigger: Meaning is obscured by poor sentence structure or unnecessary filler.

Band 4 Tips (Unclear / Rambling)
	•	“This is hard to follow.”
	•	“Say your point clearly.”
	•	“Too much waffle.”
	•	“Shorten your sentences.”
	•	“Get to the point.”

Band 5 Tips (Mostly Clear)
	•	“Reorder this for clarity.”
	•	“Lead with your main point.”
	•	“Tighten your explanation.”
	•	“Structure this more clearly.”
	•	“Clean up sentence flow.”

Band 6 Tips (Polish & Efficiency)
	•	“Clear idea — refine expression.”
	•	“Streamline wording for impact.”
	•	“Improve flow between sentences.”
	•	“Precision here earns top marks.”
	•	“Make this effortless to read.”

⸻

Implementation Note

The AI (Gemini 3 Pro) prioritises feedback categories using its internal thinkingBudget, identifying the primary limiting factor in the response before generating a single, focused tip.

Band selection is based on overall response quality, not student identity, and only one tip is delivered at a time to minimise overload and maximise immediate action.
